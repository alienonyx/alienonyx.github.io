Compute where the data lives

Conventional AI accelerators spend most of their energy shuttling data between memory and arithmetic units. Analog compute-in-memory (CIM) performs the multiply-accumulate operation in place, removing that movement and delivering substantial energy savings for inference workloads.

What we design for

  • High energy efficiency — computation in the analog domain, minimal data movement.
  • Small silicon area — dense, memory-centric arrays.
  • Inference-grade accuracy — engineered for the precision neural-network inference actually needs.
  • Standard-process compatibility — prototyped on a commercial 130 nm SiGe BiCMOS process (IHP SG13G2) using a modern open-source silicon design flow.

Alienonyx's core compute-in-memory architecture is patent-pending.
