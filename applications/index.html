Where cold photons are counted

Superconducting nanowire single-photon detectors (SNSPDs) and related superconducting detectors are the highest-performance photon counters ever built — and every field that uses them is scaling to more channels:

  • Quantum communication & networking — multi-user QKD and quantum network nodes multiply detector counts per node.
  • Photonic quantum computing — photon-number resolution is won by multiplexing, which means many detectors per optical mode.
  • Astronomy & deep-space optical communication — kilopixel-class superconducting cameras and photon-starved links.
  • Fundamental physics — dark-matter searches, particle detection, precision timing.
  • Life-science imaging — fluorescence lifetime imaging and other time-resolved methods.

In each, the readout — not the detector — is becoming the constraint on channel count, system cost, and cryogenic heat budget. That is the problem we build for.
